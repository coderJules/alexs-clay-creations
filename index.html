About Alex

Beginning at a young age, Alex was making animals out of playdough and silly puddy. With nimble fingers and focused concentration, he could turn a lifeless gray lump of silly puddy into a whimsical animal almost effortlessly.

As Alex's skill matured, he began sculpting with polymer clay. These days he creates unique clay animals in his trademark style. Alex's work has received local and nationwide recognition.
While Alex's artistic accomplishments are remarkable in their own right, what makes his story more incredible is that Alex is autistic.

When he was a little over two and half years, Alex's mom, Angelina, learned her son had autism. What followed were years of love and support, helping Alex grow as a person, and making sure his love for art was always a part of his day. Whether it was an unlimited supply of art supplies, after school art classes or supportive school staff; Alex's passion and talent was able to blossom.

Now at 18, Alex has finished high school and is focusing on his art. In addition to the the benefit of being able to earn a small income from his art sales, art still remains a source of therapy for Alex. When busy working with his hands Alex finds enjoyment and relief from his anxiety.

accolades

Washington, D.C.

Alex participated in an international competition and was one of the three hundred selected to have his art exhibited in Washington, D.C. As a result, he was featured on the front page of the Lansing State Journal.

Local

Most recently Alex's art was chosen to be part of the 17th Annual Ingham Student Art Exhibit. They were exhibited at the Lansing Art Gallery and Bloom Coffee Roasters. His paintings have also been shown throughout Michigan as part of the VSA Michigan Touring Exhibit.

Global

Alex's work has even travelled overseas! Three of his clay figures were sent to Kenya to be given as safari trip souvenirs.
